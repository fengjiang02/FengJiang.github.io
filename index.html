About Me

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at The University of Texas at Arlington, with research interests in Large Foundation Models, Multimodal Large Language Models, Graph Neural Networks, and Deep AUC Maximization. My work focuses on developing innovative solutions in machine learning with applications in bioinformatics and molecular biology.

Research

Multimodal Large Language Model Research

Currently working on LLaPA, a multimodal approach using large language models to enhance protein function prediction. This project integrates protein sequences and functional descriptions from UniProt with contrastive learning techniques. Also developed AlphaEpi for B-cell epitope prediction and Toxmm for toxicity prediction using multimodal molecular embedding.

Graph Neural Networks for TCR-Epitope Binding

Developed a novel graph-based framework for TCR-epitope binding prediction, utilizing residue interaction patterns and structural information. The work achieved state-of-the-art performance through deep AUC maximization and dynamic graph attention mechanisms.

SAM-based Auto-prompting

Created Segment Any Cell, an innovative framework enhancing the Segmentation Anything Model (SAM) for nuclei segmentation, integrating Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) within the attention layer of the Transformer.
